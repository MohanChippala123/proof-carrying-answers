Evidence-bound answers

Make every answer bring its working papers.

We have spent years trying to make models hallucinate less. That's the wrong target. You can't prove a black box won't lie. So stop trusting the generator and verify the output: decompose every answer into atomic claims, bind each to retrieved evidence, and attach a machine-checkable proof. Claims that can't be supported are withheld, not asserted.

04 / Thesis

Why this is zero-to-one

The inherited assumption

That the fix for AI lying is a more truthful model. But truthfulness is a property of a black box you cannot inspect. There is no proof to attach, only a probability to hope on.

The first-principles insight

Verifiability does not require a trustworthy generator. Decompose, retrieve, check each claim against evidence, and withhold what can't be supported. The trust moves from the model to a proof anyone can re-run.

If it succeeds

AI becomes admissible where it currently can't go: medicine, law, finance, science. Every claim arrives with a citation and a verdict. Verifiability becomes a hard requirement, the way HTTPS did.

Proof-Carrying Answers / decomposition + TF-IDF retrieval + subject/predicate entailment, implemented from first principles. Every verdict re-checks independently. Optional Claude layer for cleaner claim extraction.
